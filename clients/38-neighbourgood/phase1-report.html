Top SEO Issues Identified
All three issues stem from the same root cause: Squarespace SEO settings have not been configured at the page level
Critical

All 10 Pages Share One Generic Title

10/10 pages
100% of site - every page identical
Every page from Disandt House to the Coworking hub displays "Neighbourgood - Stay, Work & Experience Cape Town & San Francisco". Google treats each URL as a separate page - if they all look the same, none can rank for specific searches like "boutique hotel Fresnaye" or "Cape Town coworking space".
Critical

9 of 10 Pages Missing Meta Descriptions

9/10 pages
90% of site - including the ads landing page
The meta description is the first line travellers read in Google search results before deciding to click. With no descriptions set, Google generates its own snippets - usually pulling fragments of body text that don't sell your property. The homepage description is present but at 63 characters it's less than half the optimal length.
Critical

Zero H1 Headings Anywhere on the Site

10/10 pages
100% of site - no H1 found on any page
The H1 is the primary on-page signal that tells Google what each page is about. On the Disandt House page, the property name appears as an H3 - three levels below where Google expects to find the main heading. This reduces the page's topical relevance score for every "Disandt House Fresnaye" search query.

Why This Matters for Neighbourgood
A one-time Squarespace SEO setup that protects your ad spend and unlocks organic search growth

Google Can't Rank What It Can't Differentiate

When Disandt House, Ravenscraig, and Hill all have the same title and no description, Google has no way to know which page is most relevant for "boutique hotel Fresnaye" vs "Cape Town co-living". Each property needs its own unique SEO identity so Google can send the right guests to the right page.

Your Google Ads Are Paying for a Broken Landing Page

Google Ads grades your landing page for relevance to your ad keywords. A generic title that says nothing about "Disandt House" or "Fresnaye boutique hotel" earns a low Quality Score - resulting in a higher cost-per-click and lower ad position. Every day this goes unfixed, your ad budget is working harder than it needs to.

The Fix is a One-Time Platform Setup

Unlike most SEO problems, this one has a clean solution: configure Squarespace's SEO settings at the page level. Each page needs its own title (50-70 chars), description (120-160 chars), and an H1 heading. This is a one-time investment that permanently improves every page's ranking potential and your ad Quality Score.

High Upside: Starting from Zero

Neighbourgood has strong brand recognition, a compelling multi-property offering, and excellent content within each page. The SEO issue here is purely technical - the metadata layer is missing. Once properly configured, you have immediate potential to rank for "Cape Town boutique hotel", "Fresnaye accommodation", and every individual property name across 12+ listings.
