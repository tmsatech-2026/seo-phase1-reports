We noticed you are currently running Google Ads
This is exactly why we are reaching out. When you invest in Google Ads, every click costs money - and Google's Quality Score, which directly determines your cost-per-click, is partly calculated from the relevance and quality of your landing pages. Pages with thin descriptions, identical boilerplate copy, and over-long titles score lower - meaning you pay more per click for the same keywords than a competitor with well-optimised pages.

Beyond reducing your ad costs, SEO builds something Google Ads cannot: lasting organic visibility. The moment you pause your Google Ads budget, your visibility disappears. But a well-optimised page earns organic rankings that persist for months or years with no ongoing spend. Investing in SEO alongside your ads is not just smart - it protects the return on every rand you are already spending, and creates a second channel that compounds over time.

Accommodation Descriptions Are Too Thin

All six unit pages plus the Accommodation overview have descriptions of just 79-139 characters - typically a single sentence about sleeping capacity, with no mention of views, features, location, or what makes each unit distinctive.

Why it matters Accommodation pages are the highest-value pages on this site - guests comparing lodges use them to decide where to book. Thin descriptions not only rank poorly in Google, they also fail to sell the unit in search results. A 150-word unit description covering the Sabie River views, splash pool, and included amenities can directly drive more clicks and direct bookings.
Critical
6/21

Boilerplate Copy Repeated Across 6 Pages

The homepage, FAQs, Gallery, Contact, Terms and Conditions, and /index pages all share the exact same meta description word-for-word. Google treats duplicate descriptions as a quality signal problem, reducing visibility for all of these pages.

Why it matters Google's guidelines specifically warn against duplicate meta descriptions across a site. When six pages share identical text, Google cannot tell them apart in search results and will often suppress or collapse them. Each page needs a unique description that accurately reflects what that specific page contains - the Gallery description should reference photos, the Contact page should mention the booking phone number and location.
Medium
7/21

Title Tags Too Long on Accommodation Pages

Seven pages - all accommodation unit pages - have title tags of 71-87 characters. Google truncates titles at around 60 characters, cutting off the location tag entirely and leaving searchers without key context.

Why it matters The accommodation pages use a title formula of "[Unit Name] | Hazyview Cabanas | Hazyview, Mpumalanga" - but the unit names are long. "Two Bedroom Riverview Cabin with Splash Pool | Hazyview Cabanas | Hazyview, Mpumalanga" is 87 characters - Google will only show the first ~60, cutting it to "Two Bedroom Riverview Cabin with Splash Pool | Hazyvie..." The solution is shorter, keyword-focused titles: "Riverview Cabin with Pool | Hazyview Cabanas" at 44 characters with room for a differentiator.

Why This Matters for Hazyview Cabanas

Organic search and AI visibility are increasingly important for resorts competing near the Kruger National Park corridor.

Google Search - Your Highest-Intent Channel

People searching "self-catering accommodation Hazyview" or "family resort near Kruger Park" are ready to book. With 7 accommodation unit pages showing thin descriptions that mention only bed capacity, Google cannot rank them for anything beyond generic searches. Richer descriptions covering river views, splash pools, and the bush experience attract far more search traffic from guests who are actively comparing options.

ChatGPT and AI Search - The New Discovery Channel

When someone asks ChatGPT "what is the best family resort near Kruger National Park with a kids club?" the AI reads your website to decide whether to recommend Hazyview Cabanas. Max's Fun Club, the Day Spa, and the riverside setting are strong selling points - but only if each page describes these features clearly. Right now, the accommodation pages give AI almost no information to work with.

Google Ads Gets More Expensive Without Good SEO

Google's Quality Score - which sets your cost-per-click - is partly determined by how relevant and complete your landing pages are. Accommodation pages with 80-115 character descriptions are rated as thin content. Fix the descriptions and your ads land on higher-quality pages, reducing your CPC while organic traffic compounds in the background at no additional cost.

Your USPs Are There - Google Just Can't See Them

Hazyview Cabanas has strong differentiators: Sabie River frontage, a day spa, kids club with app, multiple accommodation types, and proximity to Kruger Park. But these are only mentioned on individual pages - not consistently surfaced in the metadata that Google reads first. Rewriting descriptions to lead with these USPs means every Google search result becomes a mini-advertisement for what makes this resort unique.
