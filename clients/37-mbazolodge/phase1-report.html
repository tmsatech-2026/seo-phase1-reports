Why This Matters for Mbazo Safari Lodge
The direct connection between SEO metadata and bookings

How Google Reads Your Pages

The title tag, meta description, and H1 are the three signals Google uses to understand what each page is about and rank it for relevant searches. When these are missing, duplicated, or too short, Google has less information to work with - and ranks you lower.

Duplicate Content Penalty

When two pages share the same title and description, Google must choose which one to rank. The other is often suppressed or given lower priority. Your Safari page and Dzombo Camp page are effectively competing against your own homepage and Lodge page for the same search results.

Click-Through Rate and Bookings

Your meta description is the first thing a traveller reads before deciding whether to click your result. Research shows well-written descriptions improve click-through rates by 30%+. Pages with no description get Google-generated snippets that rarely convert as well as carefully crafted ones.
