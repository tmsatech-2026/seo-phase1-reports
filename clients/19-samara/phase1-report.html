Missing H1 Headings

22 of 41 pages (54%) have no H1 heading - including the homepage, all 13 experience sub-pages, all 6 offer pages, and the contact page. Google must guess what these pages are about.

Why it matters H1 tags are the primary on-page signal Google uses to understand what a page is about. Without an H1, Google must guess - and often ranks the wrong keyword or none at all. For a luxury safari reserve, every page needs a clear H1 that includes location and category keywords.
Critical
21 pages

Meta Descriptions Too Short

21 pages have meta descriptions under 140 characters - meaning Google automatically rewrites the snippet shown in search results, often pulling irrelevant body text rather than compelling marketing copy.

Why it matters Meta descriptions are the line of text that appears in Google search results below your page title. A short description means Google writes its own - often pulling random body text that does not sell the property. Compelling descriptions directly increase click-through rates by 15-30%.

6 offer pages

Offer Page Descriptions Too Long

All 6 offer pages have meta descriptions of 260-282 characters - nearly double the 160-character ideal. Google will truncate them mid-sentence in search results, cutting off the offer details and call to action.

Why it matters Offer pages are conversion-critical. When a searcher sees your offer in Google but the description is cut off with "..." before the key detail, they are less likely to click. Each offer description should be tightly written to 140-160 characters with the core benefit and CTA front-loaded.
