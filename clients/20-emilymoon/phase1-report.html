Missing Meta Descriptions

The Gallery, Simon's Bar, Recipes, and Legend pages have no meta description. Google writes its own snippet for these pages - typically pulling random body text that doesn't sell the lodge.

Why it matters Meta descriptions are the line of text shown in Google search results below your page title. When missing, Google selects text from anywhere on the page - often pulling navigation labels or captions rather than compelling marketing copy. Research shows well-written descriptions increase click-through rates by 15-30%.

Duplicate Page Metadata

The /accommodation/ and /packages/ pages have identical titles and meta descriptions. Google cannot distinguish between them and will typically rank only one, suppressing the other entirely from search results.

Why it matters When two pages share the same metadata, Google treats them as duplicates and selects one to show in search results. Your packages page - a key conversion driver for direct bookings - may never appear in Google because it looks identical to the accommodation page. Each page needs unique, specific metadata.

Missing H1 on Homepage

Both the homepage (/) and the /home/ page have no H1 heading. Google relies on H1 tags to understand the primary topic of a page - without one on your most important page, the signal is missing entirely.

Why it matters H1 is the single most important on-page ranking signal. The homepage without an H1 means Google cannot confirm what Emily Moon River Lodge is - a hotel, a restaurant, or a venue. For a property running Google Ads, this directly affects Quality Score and the cost of every ad click.
