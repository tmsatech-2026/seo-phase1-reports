Missing H1 Headings

6 of 8 pages have no H1 tag at all. Only the homepage and privacy policy include one.

Why it matters H1 tags are the primary on-page signal Google uses to understand what a page is about. Without an H1, Google must guess — and often ranks the wrong keyword (or none at all). For luxury accommodation, every page needs a clear H1 that includes location and category keywords.

Meta Descriptions Too Short or Missing

7 of 8 pages have descriptions outside the optimal 140–160 character range, or missing entirely.

Why it matters Meta descriptions are the single line of text that appears in Google search results below your page title. A short or missing description means Google writes its own — often pulling random body text that doesn't sell the property. Compelling descriptions directly increase click-through rates by 15–30%.

Broken URL Slug — Contact Page

The Contact page URL is /swap-the-busy-city-life/ — a creative tagline, not a keyword.
