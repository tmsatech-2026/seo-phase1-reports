We noticed you are currently running Google Ads
This is exactly why we are reaching out. When you invest in Google Ads, every click costs money - and Google's Quality Score, which directly determines your cost-per-click, is partly calculated from the relevance and quality of your landing pages. Pages with thin meta descriptions and short titles score lower - meaning you pay more per click for the same keywords than a competitor with well-optimised pages.

Beyond reducing your ad costs, SEO builds something Google Ads cannot: lasting organic visibility. The moment you pause your Google Ads budget, your visibility disappears. But a well-optimised page earns organic rankings that persist for months or years with no ongoing spend. Investing in SEO alongside your ads protects the return on every rand you are already spending, and creates a second channel that compounds over time.

No Page Has an Optimised Meta Description

32 pages have no description at all and 12 have descriptions under 120 characters that simply repeat the page heading. Not one page on the site has the 120-160 character description that appears as free advertising copy in every Google search result - giving Google full control over what searchers see.

44/44

All Page Titles Too Short for Competitive Ranking

Every title uses a bare pattern such as “Athletes - Evolv Sports Global” (30 chars) that falls well short of the 50-65 character sweet spot. Athlete pages should include sport, role, and agency - for example “Corne Krige - Springbok Rugby Captain & Speaker - Evolv Sports Global” - to capture the branded searches brands actually run.
