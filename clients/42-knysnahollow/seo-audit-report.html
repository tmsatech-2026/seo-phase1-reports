Google Controls How Knysna Hollow Appears in Every Search Result

Because 12 of 14 pages have no meta description, Google ignores the missing field and generates its own search snippets from whatever body text it finds on each page. Knysna Hollow has no control over what those snippets say. A properly written 120-160 character description appears directly below the page title in every search result, making the case for why a traveller should click. Not a single key page on knysnahollow.co.za has one.

Invisible to AI Search - ChatGPT, Perplexity and Claude

When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "best boutique hotels in Knysna" or "Garden Route accommodation with a restaurant", the AI reads your website to decide whether to cite Knysna Hollow in its answer. With no meta descriptions and short page titles across 12 of 14 pages, Knysna Hollow is giving AI search very little structured signal - while competitors with well-labelled pages are being cited directly in AI answers.

Google Ads Gets More Expensive Without Good SEO

Google's Quality Score - which sets your cost-per-click - is partly determined by landing page relevance, including H1s and metadata. Poor on-page SEO means higher ad costs for the same keywords. Fix the SEO and your ads get cheaper while organic traffic compounds in the background at no extra cost.
