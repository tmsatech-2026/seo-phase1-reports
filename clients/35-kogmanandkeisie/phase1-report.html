Every Page Title Is Too Short

All 10 pages follow the pattern "[Page Name] - Kogman & Keisie Guest Farm", producing titles of 32-47 characters. Every one falls below the 50-character minimum. The pattern leaves no room for location keywords (Montagu, Langeberg, Western Cape) or property descriptors that drive search traffic.

Why it matters Page titles are the strongest on-page ranking signal. A title like "Guesthouse - Kogman & Keisie Guest Farm" (39 chars) tells Google almost nothing. Compare to "Boutique Guesthouse Montagu - Kogman & Keisie Guest Farm" (56 chars) - the expanded version would rank for "guesthouse Montagu", "boutique accommodation Western Cape", and "farm stay Langeberg" without changing anything else on the page.

Missing H1 Headings on Key Pages

Six pages - Events, About, Contact, Swimming, Greenhouse, and FAQ - have no H1 heading. These are the pages guests visit to evaluate the farm: its facilities, story, and policies. Without an H1, Google cannot determine what each page is about or which keywords to rank it for.

Why it matters H1 tags are Google's primary signal for understanding page content. The About page, for example, tells the compelling founder story of Kogman & Keisie - but without an H1, Google can't surface it for searches like "unique guest farm Western Cape" or "boutique farm Montagu owners". An H1 like "Our Story - Building Kogman & Keisie in Montagu's Langeberg Mountains" takes 30 seconds to add and immediately signals relevance.
