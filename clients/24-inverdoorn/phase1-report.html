Why it matters Title tags are the single strongest on-page ranking signal. Meta Descriptions Too Long - Google Truncates Mid-Sentence

5 pages have meta descriptions exceeding 160 characters - including About (495 chars), Contact (495 chars), Directions (494 chars), Cancellation (272 chars), and FAQs (245 chars). Google cuts these off mid-sentence in search results, showing an incomplete message to potential guests.

Why it matters When your description runs over 160 characters, Google displays it as "...and some of the most remarkable wildlife sightings..." and then stops - cutting off exactly the part that was meant to persuade the reader to click. A tight, compelling 150-160 character description increases click-through rates by 15-30%. These 5 pages need their descriptions trimmed and sharpened.

Short Descriptions and One Missing H1

The Privacy Policy page has just a 26-character description ("Effective date: 2020-01-01") and the Wildlife FAQ has only 76 characters - both well below the 120-character minimum. Additionally, the /news/ page is missing its H1 heading entirely, leaving Google without a primary signal for that page.
