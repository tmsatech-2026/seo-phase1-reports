Meta Descriptions Too Long

Around 40 of 67 pages have meta descriptions longer than 160 characters. Google cuts these off mid-sentence in search results, replacing the end with "..." - so guests searching for luxury Cape farm accommodation see an incomplete, truncated pitch for Bartholomeus Klip.

Why it matters The meta description is the first thing a potential guest reads in Google search results - before clicking. When it gets truncated, your key selling point (the farm setting, the nature reserve, the full-board experience) often disappears. Studies show well-written, complete descriptions in the 150-160 character range improve click-through rates by 15-30%.
