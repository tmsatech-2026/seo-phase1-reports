Missing H1 Headings Across Key Pages

Seven of nine pages have no H1 heading - including Accommodation, Attractions, Gallery, Rates, FAQ, Contact, and Reserve History. Only the Homepage and About page have proper H1s, leaving Google unable to determine the primary topic of most site pages.

Why it matters H1 tags are the primary on-page signal Google uses to understand what a page is about. Without an H1, Google must guess - and often ranks the wrong keyword or none at all. For a five-star safari lodge, every page needs a clear H1 that reinforces location and category keywords (e.g., "Luxury Safari Accommodation in Mjejane Game Reserve").

Auto-Generated Descriptions - Not Marketing Copy

Four pages have descriptions auto-pulled from body text rather than crafted marketing copy. The Rates page description opens with raw pricing data ("The cost is R36,750.00 per night"), the FAQ echoes the same, and Contact shows address details. The Gallery description is a list of room names (113 chars - too short).

Why it matters Meta descriptions are the first thing potential guests read in Google search results. A description that opens with raw pricing figures instead of an invitation risks deterring clicks before visitors even reach your site. Compelling, keyword-rich descriptions directly improve click-through rates by 15-30% and signal content quality to Google's systems.

Short Page Titles Missing Keyword Opportunities

Four pages have titles below the 50-character optimal range: FAQ (37 chars), Gallery (41 chars), Contact (44 chars), and Reserve History (48 chars). These short titles fail to include location and category keywords that could drive additional organic traffic.

Why it matters Page titles are the strongest on-page ranking signal. A title like "FAQ - Fifty-Five Mjejane - Game Lodge" wastes the opportunity to target searches like "Mjejane game reserve lodge FAQ" or "five-star safari lodge rates Kruger". Expanding titles to 55-65 characters with relevant keywords can meaningfully lift page rankings.
